# Holdfast — Brand Guide

*v0.1 · July 2026*

---

## 1. The idea

Every other blocker is polite. It asks nicely, then folds the moment you push back. Holdfast doesn't. The brand is **tough love, made honest**: it's on your side, but it will not be talked out of it. Blunt, dry, a little funny, never cruel. The kind of friend who says "no" and means it — and you thank them later.

**Brand promise:** *The blocker you can't talk your way out of.*

---

## 2. Name

**Holdfast.** Committed (it's the product and the repo).

- **Strength:** memorable, on-brand, and it stands out in an app store full of earnest "Focus / Freedom / Flow" names. It *tells you what it does to you.*
- **Known constraint:** the word "holdfast" is a hurdle for the SME/enterprise phase — an office manager won't expense "Holdfast," and app stores / procurement get twitchy about mild profanity.
- **Resolution (recommended):** keep **Holdfast** as the consumer-facing brand, and reserve a clean B2B brand for the SME tier running the *same engine* (candidates: **Holdfast**, **Hardline**, **Ironclad**). Decide before the SME launch, not now. For the POC and individual product, Holdfast is exactly right.

---

## 3. Voice & tone

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Be blunt: "You blocked this. Sit with it." | Be preachy or shaming |
| Be dry-funny: "Nice try." | Be cruel or insulting |
| Be on the user's side: "Past-you asked me to hold the line." | Be a nanny |
| Use short, hard sentences | Hedge or over-explain |
| Respect the user's intelligence | Use corporate wellness jargon ("mindful digital journey") |

**Voice test:** if a sentence could appear in a meditation app, rewrite it harder.

**Microcopy examples**
- Blocked page headline: **"Blocked. On purpose."**
- Cooldown wait: *"You set a 20-minute wait. 18:42 to go. Past-you was serious."*
- Reason box: *"Tell me why. In writing."*
- Relapse stat: *"4th time this week. Average visit: 47 minutes."*
- Unblock confirm: *"Still want in? The clock doesn't care, but I'll let you."*

## 4. Logo — a two-mark system

The name is literally *hard as a rock*, so the consumer brand gets a mascot; the B2B brand keeps the serious glyph. Two marks, one family, same Redline tile.

**Consumer — "Rocky", the holdfast rock.** A cute-but-tough granite pebble with a stern brow and a flat, determined mouth, sitting on the Redline tile. Grumpy, not angry; on your side, but immovable. This is the face of **Holdfast** — memorable, sticker-able, and it makes "the block that won't budge" adorable instead of hostile. Speckle texture on the stone ties to the granite texture (§7a).

**B2B — the holdfast.** The original geometric glyph — a rounded housing with a bar thrown across it — stays as the mark for **Holdfast for Teams**. Serious, procurement-friendly, "locked, deliberately."

**Wordmark:** `HARDASS` in a heavy grotesque, tight tracking, all caps; `BLOCKER` lighter/smaller beneath or trailing. The weight contrast *is* the attitude.

**Do:** keep the mascot on the Redline tile; keep the face simple enough to read at 16px. Use the holdfast for anything B2B.
**Don't:** make Rocky *smile* (he's stoic, not cheerful), add gradients, or outline the wordmark.

## 5. Colour palette

The palette is **near-black + one hot accent**. Restraint reads as seriousness; the single hot colour is the "line you don't cross."

| Role | Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base | Ink | `#0E0E10` | Backgrounds, blocked page |
| Surface | Slate | `#1B1C20` | Cards, panels |
| Line | Steel | `#33353B` | Borders, dividers |
| Text | Bone | `#F4F1EC` | Primary text on dark |
| Muted | Ash | `#9A9CA3` | Secondary text |
| **Accent** | **Redline** | **`#FF3B30`** | The block, the bar, the "no" |
| Signal | Amber | `#FFB800` | Cooldown timer, warnings |
| Success | Clear | `#37D67A` | Rare — "you held the line" |
| Stone | Granite | `#A7ABB2` | Rocky's body, texture base |
| Stone dark | Basalt | `#7C8188` | Rock speckle, texture flecks |

### 7a. Texture — granite

The brand has one texture: **granite speckle**. It's the visual echo of "hard as rock" and it's what stops the near-black palette feeling like every other dark SaaS.

- **What it is:** a near-black (Ink) base scattered with faint Basalt/Ash flecks — like polished stone. Subtle, never loud; you should feel it before you notice it.
- **Where it goes:** hero surfaces (the blocked page background, landing hero, brand boards). Not behind body text or forms — legibility first.
- **How to build it:** layered sparse dots (small `radial-gradient` speckles or a tiled SVG of tiny circles at ~4–8% opacity in Basalt/Ash over Ink). Flat, matte — no glossy or 3D rock. Keep contrast low so text stays readable on top.
- **Don't:** photographic rock textures, bevels, drop shadows, or anything that reads "stock granite countertop." Matte speckle only.

Accent is used *sparingly* — it's the stop sign, not the wallpaper.

## 6. Typography

- **Display / wordmark:** a heavy grotesque — **Archivo Black**, **Anton**, or **Space Grotesk (Bold)**. Tight, loud, confident.
- **UI / body:** **Inter** (or system-ui). Neutral, legible, gets out of the way.
- **Numerals (Cooldown timer):** tabular figures, large. The countdown is a hero element — treat it like a scoreboard.

Rule: **one loud typeface, one quiet one.** Never two loud.

---

## 7. Design principles

1. **Friction is the feature.** The UI should feel a touch heavy on unblock actions and effortless on block actions. Make the right thing easy and the wrong thing deliberate.
2. **No dark patterns in reverse.** We add friction to *unblocking*, never trick the user or hide the exit. The block is honest and always escapable — just slowly, with reflection.
3. **Show the mirror.** Relapse history and stats are core UI, not buried. The product's power is reflection, not punishment.
4. **Serious, not grim.** Dry humour keeps it human. Tough love, not a prison.
5. **Legible under temptation.** At the moment of craving, the user is impatient — high contrast, big countdown, one clear action.

---

## 8. Applications

- **Blocked page:** Ink background, Redline bar, big Bone headline ("Blocked. On purpose."), Amber Cooldown countdown, small Ash relapse stats. The flagship brand surface — get this right first.
- **Extension icon:** solid holdfast glyph, Redline on Ink.
- **SME console (Phase 2):** dial the humour down, keep the palette; swap to the clean B2B name. Same bones, adult-in-the-room tone.

---

*Brand exists to make one promise legible everywhere: this one won't fold.*

Hardass Blocker — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 9 July 2026

Hardass Blocker is a website blocker. This policy explains, in plain language, exactly what data the extension handles and where it goes. The short version: in personal use, all of your data stays on your own device and nothing is transmitted to us or to any third party.

1. What the extension stores

Hardass Blocker keeps a small amount of data in your browser's local storage (chrome.storage.local) so it can do its job:

DataPurpose
Your blocklist (the domains you choose to block)To know which sites to block.
Your settings (cooldown length, allowance length, etc.)To apply the rules you configured.
Cooldown timers and temporary allowancesTo enforce the waiting period before a block can be lifted.
Your relapse log — timestamps and the short reasons you type when unblocking a siteTo show you your own history so you can reflect on it. This is shown only to you.

This data lives on your device. In personal (unmanaged) use it is never sent to us, and we do not operate any server that receives it. We have no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no tracking.

2. What the extension does not do

  • It does not read, collect, or transmit the content of the pages you visit.
  • It does not track or log your general browsing history.
  • It does not sell, rent, or share any data with third parties.
  • It does not use your data for advertising, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to blocking.

The extension requests access to all sites only so its blocking rules can redirect a blocked page to its own "blocked" screen. It inspects only the address of the page you are navigating to, and only to decide whether that address is on your blocklist. It does not open, read, or store the contents of any page.

3. Managed (team / enterprise) mode

Hardass Blocker can optionally be enrolled into a managed mode by an organisation (for example, an employer or a program you have joined). This only happens if you or your administrator explicitly enrol the extension against a server address that your organisation controls.

When enrolled in managed mode, the extension communicates with that organisation's own server — not with us — to:

  • fetch the blocking policy set by your administrator;
  • send access requests you submit (the domain and the reason you provide); and
  • send basic enforcement events (for example, that a blocked domain was accessed or allowed).

In managed mode, how that data is used and retained is governed by the enrolling organisation's own privacy policy. Hardass Blocker acts only as the software that connects your browser to the server your organisation runs. If you are not part of a managed deployment, none of this applies to you and no data leaves your device.

4. Data retention and deletion

Because personal-mode data is stored locally, you are in full control of it. Removing a blocked site clears its associated timers and allowances. Uninstalling the extension permanently deletes all locally stored data. To leave managed mode, use the extension's options page (subject to any lock your administrator has set).

5. Permissions

The extension requests only the permissions it needs to block sites:

PermissionWhy
declarativeNetRequestTo block and redirect requests to sites on your blocklist. This runs on rules; it does not report your browsing to the extension or to us.
storageTo save your blocklist, settings, and history locally on your device.
alarmsTo run cooldown/allowance timers and to periodically re-check that your blocking rules are still in place.
Host access (<all_urls>)So blocking rules can apply to any site you choose to block. The extension does not read page content.

6. Children

Hardass Blocker is a general-purpose productivity tool and is not directed at children.

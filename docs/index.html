The Cooldown

To lift any block, you do all three. The wait kills the impulse; the reason kills the self-deception; the history is the mirror.

1

Wait

A mandatory cooldown (you set the length — default 20 min). It keeps running even if you close the tab. Reopening doesn't reset it.

2

Write why

Type a real reason before you can unblock. Minimum length enforced. No blank, thoughtless unblocks.

3

Face your history

See how many times you've caved this week, your average self-granted pass, and when you last gave in.

What you get

Real blocking

Blocked sites redirect to the Cooldown — subdomains and http/https covered.

Time-boxed passes

Unblock grants a few minutes, then the site re-blocks itself automatically.

No side doors

Translation proxies, cached copies, and archive mirrors of blocked sites are blocked too. "Nice try."

Your history, kept honest

Every unblock is logged with the reason you typed — and shown back to you.

Straight about what it is

Holdfast is built for self-discipline, not surveillance. Your blocklist, settings, history, and browsing stay on your device — never uploaded, no ads, no trackers, no analytics. The only exception is optional: if you choose Holdfast Pro, your email and subscription status go to our account server to unlock it (payments via Stripe). That's it — your browsing is never read or sent, free or Pro.

And we won't oversell it: on your own machine, a determined person can still turn an extension off. Holdfast raises the cost of caving with the Cooldown — the friction that changes behaviour. Past-you set the rule. Holdfast holds the line.
